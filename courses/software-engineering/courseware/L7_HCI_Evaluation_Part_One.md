# L7. HCI Evaluation Part One

## HCI Evaluation

Evaluation is a crucial part of the user-centred development process – we want to ensure our software meets our users' requirements

The focus of this lecture is on the Think Aloud technique and Heuristic Evaluation, which are two of the most widely used evaluation methods in industry

## The Think Aloud evaluation technique

Users are asked to verbalise (表达) what they are thinking and doing as they perform a task using your software

The Think Aloud technique provides insights into the user experience of using your software

It can identify issues with the software e.g. navigation problems or content that can be improved

It can be used as part of the software development process to iteratively improve software or used with a finished product

## Benefits of Think Aloud

- Cheap
- Relatively easy
- It provides insight into people's experiences as they interact with your product
- It can be carried out with low numbers of participants
- Fits in (适合) with most software development processes

## Drawbacks of Think Aloud

- it relies on people verbalising (口头表达) their thoughts and impressions, rather than objective measures (客观的衡量标准)
- Participants may say what they believe to be the right answer, rather than what they really think (social desirability bias). This can distort (扭曲) your results and conclusions

## Planning a Think Aloud evaluation

- Decide what questions you want your study to answer. For example, whether users can find particular content or what their understanding is of the information presented. 他们对所呈现信息的理解程度
- Write down the tasks you want the user to complete while using your software
- Decide how many participants you want to recruit (招募) and how long you want the sessions to last (15 to 60 minutes works well)

## Carrying out a Think Aloud evaluation 1

- Have a facilitator (引导员) to run the evaluation and one or two observers to take notes on what the user says
- The facilitator explains to the participants how a think aloud works: they should tell you their thoughts, reactions and emotions as they occur while they are performing the task
- Explain that there is no right answer and it’s fine to be critical

## Carrying out a Think Aloud evaluation 2

- Ask the participants to complete the tasks you have planned. This should be uninterrupted (不中断) as far as possible, although the facilitator will probably need to give some prompts.
- If the user goes silent (this will happen quite often) then prompt them to verbalise (表达) their thoughts by asking “what are you thinking?”.

## Analysing a Think Aloud evaluation

- Collect the written notes from both observers and put them into one document.
- Organise the notes into meaningful categories e.g. what features helped users; what features led to problems; any additional features that users wanted.
- You can make your own meaningful categories (分类)
- Count the number of times users comment about different categories to identify the biggest issues.

## What is a heuristic?

- A generalization or rule of thumb 一个推广或经验法则
- Experienced-based strategies that enable you to make a decision quickly 基于经验的策略

- However, the decision might not be optimal（最优）!
- Some examples include:
  - choosing a familiar brand when shopping
  - judging someone's profession based on their appearance
  - selecting the first ‘good enough’ option when making a choice

## Heuristic evaluation 1

- An evaluation technique conducted without users
- Also known as expert evaluation as it’s sometimes carried out by external experts (sometimes by the development team) aka evaluators 也称为评估员
- It’s a type of analytical evaluation, that is, based on a set of principles or a model... 这是一种基于一套原则或模型的分析评估......
- ...rather than by observing users (which is known as empirical evaluation 实证评估)

## Heuristic evaluation 2

It’s an inspection（检查）method – it involves inspecting a design to find usability（可用性）problems

This involves asking whether the design complies with usability principles (a set of heuristics) 设计是否符合可用性原则（一套启发式方法）

## Heuristic evaluation is widely used because...

- It’s cheap (only needs a small number of evaluators and no specialist equipment or labs) 不需要专业设备或实验室
- Relatively easy to carry out (can do it after a few hours of training) 经过几个小时训练就能完成
- Instant gratification – lists of problems are available immediately after the inspection 即时满足——检查后立即提供问题清单
- It fits in（适合）with most software development processes used in industry
- It’s a very cost effective: benefit-cost ratio of 48: e.g. cost of $10,500; expected benefits $500,000 (Nielsen 1994). 它的成本效益非常高：效益成本比为48：例如成本为10,500美元；预期收益为50万美元

## Where are the users?

- Heuristic evaluation is based on HCI researchers' extensive experience of designing and evaluating interfaces 启发式评估基于HCI研究者在设计和评估界面方面的丰富经验
- By focusing on users, HCI researchers learned what works（有效） and what doesn't
- Their experience is distilled into（浓缩为） usability principles (a set of heuristics)
- The principles represent the findings from thousands of user studies
- They have been used for over 30 years

## Visibility of system status - feedback

- Does the design clearly communicate its state? 设计是否清晰传达了其状态?
- Is feedback presented quickly after user actions? 用户操作后反馈是否迅速呈现?
- Inform the user about what’s going on:
  - show appropriate feedback and progress
  - do not show blank screens 请勿显示空白屏幕
  - do not show static “load” or progress messages 不要显示静态的“加载”或进度信息

## Visibility of system status: examples

- Microsoft Live
  Password strength is shown as the password is entered. Colors are used to augment the message. 增强信息
- Tick
  A feedback message is displayed when an action is performed 执行动作时会显示反馈消息

## Match between system and real world - conventions 系统与现实世界的匹配——约定

- Will user be familiar with the terminology（术语） used in the design?
- Do the design’s controls follow real-world conventions? 设计中的操作是否遵循现实世界的惯例?
- There must be a match between the system’s interface controls and the real world 系统界面控制必须与现实世界保持一致
- The system should speak the users’ language, with words, phrases（短语） and concepts familiar to the user, rather than system-oriented terms（非系统导向的术语）
- Follow real-world conventions, making information appear in a natural and logical order 遵循现实世界的惯例，使信息以自然且合乎逻辑的顺序呈现

## Match between system and real world - examples

- iTunes

Organized as a library that contains your media library: music, movies, TV shows, audiobooks. Beneath the Library is the Store where you can buy more media to put in your Library. 以包含你的媒体库的图书馆形式组织：音乐、电影、电视剧、有声书。图书馆下方是商店，你可以在那里购买更多媒体，放入你的图书馆。

## User control and freedom – emergency exits

- Does the design allow users to go back a step in the process? 设计是否允许用户回溯流程中的某个步骤?
- Are exit links easily discoverable? 退出链接容易被发现吗?
- Users often choose system functions by mistake and will need a clearly marked ‘emergency exit’ to leave the unwanted state without having to go through an extended dialog. 用户经常错误地选择系统功能，需要明确标记的“紧急出口”才能离开不想要的状态，而无需经历冗长的对话。
- Support undo and redo and a clear way to navigate. 支持撤销和重做，并提供清晰的导航方式。
- Provide bread crumbs to clearly show where the user is. 提供线索，清楚显示用户所在位置。

## User control and freedom - examples

- Wufoo

Clearly marks where the person is and where they can go by showing the selection in each menu

- Balsamiq

Undo and Redo buttons are available in the toolbar, and can also be accessed with the standard keyboard shortcuts

## Consistency and standards

- Does the design follow industry conventions? 设计是否遵循行业惯例?
- Are visual treatments used consistently throughout the design? 视觉处理在整个设计中是否始终如一地使用?
- Users should not have to wonder whether different words, situations, or actions

mean the same thing. 用户不应担心不同的词语、情境或行为是否含义相同。
- Follow platform conventions.

## Consistency: examples

- Gmail

When Gmail was designed, they based the organizational folders on the same ones used in other client email applications: Inbox, Drafts, Sent Mail. 组织文件夹基于其他客户端邮件应用中使用的文件夹：收件箱、草稿、已发送邮件。

- Microsoft Office

Word, Excel, and PowerPoint all use the same style toolbar（相同的样式工具栏） with the same primary menu options（主菜单选择）: Home, Insert, Page Layout.

## Error prevention

- Does the design prevent slips by using helpful constraints? 设计是否通过使用有用的约束来防止滑动？

- Does the design warn users before they perform risky actions?

- Even better than good error messages is a careful design which prevents a problem from occurring in the first place. 比起良好的错误信息，更好的是精心设计，防止问题发生。

- Either eliminate error-prone conditions or check for them and present users with a confirmation option before they commit to the action. 要么消除易出错的条件，要么检查它们，并在用户承诺操作前提供确认选项。

## Error prevention: examples

- Yammer

Disables the update button after it is clicked, so the person cannot update the post twice by accident 点击更新按钮后关闭，这样用户就不会意外重复更新帖子

- Example from “Web form Design:Filling in the Blanks” by Luke W.

Make the primary action prominent with a larger click area. Cancel and other secondary actions are just shown as links 让主要动作突出，并扩大点击面积。取消和其他辅助动作只是以链接形式显示

## Recognition rather than recall

- Does the design keep important information visible, so that users do not have to memorize it?
- Does the design offer help in-context?
- Minimize the user's memory load.
- Make objects, actions, and options visible.
- The user should not have to remember information from one part of the dialogue to another. 用户不应需要记住对话中各部分的信息。
- Instructions for use of the system should be visible or easily retrievable whenever appropriate. 系统的使用说明应在适当时可见或易于检索。

## Recognition: examples

- Quanta IDE

  Auto completion for coding in a development environment 开发环境中的自动完成编码

- Keynote

  Previews the fonts you can pick from, instead of just the font name 预览你可以选择的字体，而不仅仅是字体名称

## Flexibility and efficiency of use

- Does the design provide accelerators like keyboard shortcuts and touch gestures? 设计中是否提供加速器，比如键盘快捷键和触控手势？
- Can individual users customise content and/or functionality? 个别用户可以自定义内容和/或功能吗？
- Accelerators — unseen by the novice user — may often speed up the interaction for the expert user so that the system can cater to both inexperienced and experienced users 新手用户看不到——通常能加快专家用户的交互速度，使系统能够同时满足新手和有经验的用户需求
- Allow users to tailor frequent actions

## Aesthetic and minimalist design

- Is the visual design and content focused on the essentials?
- Have all distracting, unnecessary elements been removed? 所有分散注意力、不必要的元素都被移除了吗?
- Dialogues should not contain information which is irrelevant or rarely needed
- Every extra unit of information in a dialogue competes with the relevant units of information and diminishes their relative visibility 对话中每多出的信息单位都会与相关信息单位竞争，降低它们的相对可见性
- Visual layout should respect the principles of contrast, repetition, alignment, and proximity. 视觉布局应尊重对比、重复、对齐和接近的原则。

## Minimalist design: example

Kontain’s search menu exemplifies the four principles of visual design （Kontain的搜索菜单体现了视觉设计的四大原则）：

1. Contrast: bold text is used for the two labels in the search
2. Repetition: the orange, blue, and green text match the media types
3. Alignment : strong left alignment of text, right aligned drop down
4. Proximity: a light rule is used to separate tags from the other options

对比：搜索中的两个标签使用加粗文字

重复：橙色、蓝色和绿色文本对应媒体类型

对齐：文本强烈左对齐，右下拉选单

接近性：使用光线规则将标签与其他选项区分开

## Help users recognise, diagnose (诊断) and recover from errors

- Does the design use traditional error message visuals, like bold, red text?
- Does the design offer a solution that solves the error immediately?
- Help users recognize, diagnose, and recover from errors.

- Error messages should be expressed in plain language (no jargon), precisely indicate the problem, and constructively suggest a solution. 错误信息应用通俗易懂的语言表达（无行话），准确指出问题，并建设性地提出解决方案。

## Error recognition and recovery: examples

- Digg

  Provides immediate feedback with specific instructions

- Humorous ‘Page Not Found’ Error

  Uses a funny image and text, but provides viable alternatives（可行的替代方案）(article listings and blog link) and a course of action（行动方案）(report it)

## Help and documentation

- Is help documentation easy to search?

- Is help provided in context right at the moment when the user requires it?

- Even though it is better if software can be used without documentation, it may be necessary to provide help and documentation.

- Any such information should be contextual, easy to search, focused on the user’s task, list concrete steps to be carried out, and not be too large. 任何此类信息应具上下文性，易于搜索，聚焦用户任务，列出具体步骤，且内容不应过大。

  Contextual tips in Picnik are clear and easy to navigate 上下文提示清晰易懂

- GoodBarry

  Embedded videos can be used to showcase features as well as get people started using the product

## How to run a heuristic evaluation 1

- Each of the 3 – 5 evaluators does a heuristic evaluation of an interface alone 3到5位评估者各自单独对一个界面进行启发式评估
- Sometimes a facilitator can record an evaluator’s comments, sometimes the evaluator does it

- A facilitator can answer evaluators’ questions, in contrast to traditional user testing, particularly if it’s not a walk up and use system 与传统用户测试不同，主持人可以回答评估者的问题，尤其是在非现场使用系统的情况下
- Heuristic evaluation can be done on paper prototypes

## How to run a heuristic evaluation 2

- Heuristic evaluations typically last 1 – 2 hours, but it does depend on the complexity of the software
- The expert goes through the interface several times – first time to get a feel for the system, second time to focus on specific elements
- Evaluators can be given scenarios that describe typical usage scenarios 评估者可以被分配描述典型使用场景的场景
- Evaluators produce a list of usability problems: the usability principle and the design feature that violated it 评估者会列出可用性问题：可用性原则及其违背的设计特征

## Benefits of heuristic evaluation

- Cheap
- Relatively easy
- Instant gratification lists of problems are available immediately after the inspection 检查后立即提供即时问题清单
- It can be carried out with low numbers of participants
- Fits in with most software development processes
- Cost effective

## Drawbacks of heuristic evaluation

- Important issues may get missed
- Might identify false issues
- Many trivial issues are often identified, making it seem overly critical 许多琐碎的问题经常被指出，使得它显得过于苛刻
- Experts have biases
